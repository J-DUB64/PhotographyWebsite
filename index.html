"The Importance of High Quality Fine Art Photography"

"By Joseph Wankelman. Published" January 3rd

Fine art photography is a medium that allows photographers to express their creativity and personal vision. High quality fine art photography is not just beautiful to look at, but it also has the power to evoke emotions, tell stories, and spark conversations. One of the most important aspects of fine art photography is the technical skill and attention to detail that goes into creating a high quality image. This includes factors such as composition, lighting, and post-processing. A photograph that is well composed and expertly lit will immediately draw the viewer's eye and hold their attention. Another important aspect of fine art photography is the artist's personal vision and style. A fine art photographer's body of work should have a cohesive style and aesthetic that is uniquely their own. This allows the viewer to not only appreciate the beauty of the individual images, but also to gain a deeper understanding of the photographer's perspective and the message they are trying to convey. Additionally, high quality fine art photography can also be a valuable investment. Not only do these images have the potential to appreciate in value over time, but they also have the ability to enhance the aesthetic of any space they are displayed in.

Written by Joseph Wankelman

The Power of Street Photography

"By Joseph Wankelman. Published" January 3rd

Street photography is a genre of photography that captures candid and unplanned moments in public spaces. It is a powerful medium that allows photographers to document the world around them and tell stories about the human condition in an authentic and emotionally charged way. Street photographers must be able to anticipate and respond quickly to unfolding events, and create a sense of place. Street photography also has the power to raise awareness and spark conversations about social and political issues. It requires a keen sense of observation and the ability to anticipate and respond quickly to unfolding events.
